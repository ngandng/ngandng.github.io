This paper presents a novel mission planning framework for multi-robot systems operating in unknown environments. The system consists of a master vehicle that carries a fleet of robots that are not required to be homogeneous. The mission is to coordinate the robots approaching and performing tasks at a set of targets, then returning to the master vehicle using positional information relayed through the communication module. All tasks remain unknown until they enter the sensing range of the master vehicle. Our strategy focuses on managing energy constraints for the robots through an efficient task assignment procedure while maximizing the number of tasks that need to be fulfilled. We evaluated the proposed method across various scenarios, demonstrating its ability to generate energy-efficient plans and highlighting the quality of the planning process.
